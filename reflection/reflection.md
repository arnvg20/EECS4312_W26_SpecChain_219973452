# Application: Wysa

- Most important difference between pipelines:  
The automated pipeline produced higher review coverage and faster artifact generation, while the manual pipeline produced more precise and focused personas, requirements, and tests. The hybrid pipeline combined both strengths by preserving automation speed while improving clarity and reducing ambiguity through manual refinement.

- Most useful pipeline:  
The hybrid pipeline produced the most useful outputs because it maintained the broader review coverage of the automated pipeline while improving the clarity, traceability, and testability of the generated personas, requirements, and validation tests.

- Most surprising finding:  
The automated pipeline was able to generate meaningful review groups and personas very quickly, but many of the initial requirements used vague phrases such as “effective support” and “easy to use,” which made them harder to validate until manually refined.

- Observed weakness in the automated pipeline:  
Some automated personas and requirements included vague wording and unsupported assumptions, and several validation scenarios were too general before refinement. This increased the ambiguity ratio compared to the manual and hybrid pipelines.
